Visualising Trees around Melbourne

Trees in the city of Melbourne

Age Diversity

Trees have a few stages of life them being

  • New Trees who were very recently planted.
  • Juvenile Trees who are young trees that were recently planted in the last few years.
  • Semi-mature Trees who are trees that are no longer young however have not fully matured.
  • Mature Trees who are trees that have fully matured.
  • Over-mature Trees who are trees that have gone pass the mature stage and begun to die.
  • Unknown Maturity Trees who are trees with unknown maturities.

As trees get older and become over-mature they must be removed to allow new trees to be planted and grow. However as many of the trees in Melbourne were planted at the same time they must all be removed at the same time. Hence it is important to have many trees with a vareity of ages.




Species Diversity

Having a wide variety of trees is also important as it can allow for increased strength against pests, diseases and stress.

Unfortunately the trees planted in Melbourne are dominated by Eucalyptus, Acacias, Ulmus (Elms) and Platanus (Planes).

As mentioned before with many of the trees being planted at similar times many of the mature trees are reaching the end of their life and becoming over-mature hence it is important to have a vareity of types.

Significant Trees in the city of Yarra


Significant Trees Program in Yarra

Within the city of Yarra there is a program that allows anyone to nominate a tree to be put on the significant tree register. These trees are in various places such as parks, streets, residential areas, industrial areas and other locations.

These trees may be classified as significant for many reasons such as having an impressive size, age, rarity or even ecological or cultural signficance.

This registery of significant trees allows the council to easily identify trees that has some sort of additional value. This helps when planning, managing and conversving the nature in the city. It also allows for the community to become more aware and appreciative of these significant trees that are all around them.












Significant Tree Diversity

Unfortunately it seems that most of the significant trees in the city of Yarra suffer from the same problem that being majority are mature Eucalyptus and Ulmus (Elms). However this is understandable as due to the fact that these types of trees tend to grow faster than the others.
